🔍 What Worked (And What Didn't)

High-Performance Content Types

Top Performers:

  • Data-backed controversy: Posts with statistics + controversial takes got 3x engagement
  • Industry drama analysis: CEO/company decisions sparked 2.5x more comments
  • Twitter threads: Multi-tweet narratives outperformed single tweets by 4x
  • LinkedIn hot takes: Professional controversy generated highest engagement rates

Learning: Platform Context Is Everything

The same content performed drastically differently across platforms:

  • Twitter: Loved quick, punchy takes with data snippets
  • LinkedIn: Wanted longer-form professional analysis
  • Blog: Preferred comprehensive deep-dives with actionable insights

⚠️ Challenges & Lessons Learned

Not everything went according to plan. Here's what we learned from the struggles:

Growth Challenges

  • Follower Growth (3% vs 100% target): Quality engagement didn't translate to follower growth as expected
  • Blog Traffic (12% vs 100% target): SEO takes time; social traffic was inconsistent
  • Cross-platform consistency: Each platform required unique content adaptation

Strategic Adjustments Made

  1. Increased controversy ratio: Moved from 20% to 40% controversial content
  2. Platform-native posting: Stopped cross-posting; created unique content per platform
  3. Timing optimization: Shifted posting schedule based on engagement data
  4. Community engagement: Started actively participating in discussions vs. just broadcasting
